Catch the events & freeze the timestamp

Detection is estimate-level and runs as three SQL functions; discover_pending_conversions() calls them and freezes each result into gads_conversion_uploads once (ON CONFLICT (estimate_id, conversion_type) DO NOTHING). Green = immutable timestamp (stable after the fact); amber = mutable field (recomputed every discovery run until the row is frozen). All dates stored UTC.

Converted leadsid 6794281105 · converted_leadConverted
IN conversions · primaryMANY_PER_CLICK 		The sale — at least one estimate option was approved (or pro-approved) by the customer. get_pending_converted_lead_conversions().
Gate: EXISTS an estimate_options row with approval_status IN ('approved','pro approved'). No linked-Job requirement (job_id returned NULL).
Anchor timestamp: MAX(estimate_options.updated_at) over the approved options (mutable).
Value: SUM(estimate_options.total_amount) / 100 over the approved options.
GCLID: oldest customer_gclids.gclid with first_seen_at ≥ MAX(approved updated_at) − 90 days.		 The only action that counts toward bidding (include_in_conversions + primary_for_goal). job.created_at is not used — the anchor is the mutable approved-option updated_at. total_amount is in cents → divide by 100. Value is the sum of all approved options, not a single option.

How the row reaches Google (shared pipeline): discover_pending_conversions() runs the three functions above and inserts each result into gads_conversion_uploads as status='pending' / lifecycle='queued', freezing the gclid + timestamp + value at that moment. The google-ads-conversion-upload function then drains queued/retrying rows: it attaches hashed email/phone (Enhanced Conversions) plus the GCLID when present, formats conversion_date_time in UTC (+00:00), and expires any row whose timestamp is older than 90 days before it is sent. Per-row Google responses (accepted / per-error) are stored back on the upload row and its batch.
