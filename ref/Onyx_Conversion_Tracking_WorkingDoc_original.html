The whole system in five steps

STEP 1
Catch the events
Detect when an estimate is scheduled, done, and approved/converted.
STEP 2
Freeze the timestamp
Decide the correct status + date for each; snapshot the mutable ones.
STEP 3
Link the lead source
Tie each conversion back to where the lead came from.
STEP 4
Send & confirm
Upload to Google Ads and verify it was accepted, no silent fails.
STEP 5
Monitor for gaps
Flag when a conversion should exist but didn't arrive.
1

Catch the events & freeze the timestamp

The three Google Ads actions, mapped to the exact Housecall Pro API field that fires each. Field paths and example values are taken from the live estimates payload (response-2.json, 7,761 estimates). Green = immutable date (trustworthy retroactively); amber = mutable, must be snapshotted on first sighting.

Google Ads action What it means in real life How we catch it in HCP (exact fields) Comments — watch for
BOOKING_CONFIRMEDid 7576326162Scheduled The estimate is on the dispatch board, scheduled, and assigned to a technician — the estimate is successfully booked and we're sending a tech out. options[].status = "scheduled" AND assigned_employees[] non-empty (a tech is assigned).
Conversion timestamp: booked_at = the moment our sync first observes that scheduled + assigned state (snapshot observed_at). Validated: created_at is the estimate's birth, often earlier than the booking (e.g. #7760 created 20:38:46 but the record changed at 20:49:12); scheduled_start is the future appointment (up to 6.7 days out in this sample). Neither marks the booking moment.
Use created_at only as a fallback when created_at == updated_at (estimate created already-scheduled in one step).		 The "how we catch it" logic at left is the proposed concept — to be validated in implementation.
Still to validate (HCP side): that updated_at moves when status → scheduled, and which exact timestamp to send. Confirm by watching one estimate across two syncs. scheduled_start = appointment date (mutable), not the conversion time.
Qualified leadsid 6802897127Qualified A qualified lead: the customer was visited by a technician and received an estimate from us — real value and genuine potential, not just a booking. options[].status ∈ {"complete rated", "complete unrated", "created job from estimate"}, OR an option was approved on-site.
Anchor timestamp: first available in the fallback chain → sent-snapshot → approved-snapshot → completed_at → started_at → on_my_way_at → created_at (see Comments).
e.g. est #7759 — approved on the job, never sent, completed_at: null → anchor falls back to approved-snapshot (updated_at 2026-06-01T20:46:13Z), or started_at 2026-06-01T20:31:06Z if no snapshot exists yet.		 Anchor = first available, in this fallback order (sent-first, per business definition "estimate presented"):
1. sent snapshot — updated_at when status → submitted for signoff (the quote was presented — primary anchor)
2. approved snapshot — updated_at when approval_status → approved / pro approved (covers "approved on the job, never sent")
3. completed_at — visit finished
4. started_at — tech began on-site (#7759 has this; completed_at null)
5. on_my_way_at — tech en route
6. option.created_at — last resort.
Watch: no real sent_at/approved_at exists — the top two anchors are snapshots only, so sent-first requires the snapshot tracker to be live. Until then it falls through to the real visit timestamps. Also not counted, MANY_PER_CLICK, $250 default.
Converted leadsid 6794281105Converted The sale — customer said yes and a real job was created with revenue attached. options[].approval_status ∈ {"approved", "pro approved"} AND a linked Job (job_…) exists.
Anchor timestamp: job.created_at (immutable).
Value: options[].total_amount ÷ 100.
e.g. est #7759 — approval_status: "pro approved", total_amount: 405000 → $4,050.00		 The only action that counts toward bidding. total_amount is in cents — divide by 100. Confirm a real Job ID — not status "created job from estimate" alone (2,102 rows vs 358 with a Job ID).

Confirmed field paths (live payload): estimate root id = csr_…; work_status; schedule.scheduled_start / scheduled_end (= future appointment slot, not a conversion time); work_timestamps.{on_my_way_at, started_at, completed_at}; lead_source. Per option in options[]: id = est_…, status, approval_status, total_amount (cents), created_at, updated_at. Dates are ISO-8601 UTC (…Z) — store UTC, report in ET. Rule (validated against live API): a conversion's timestamp is when the status transition is first observed (snapshot), never scheduled_start, and not created_at when it differs from updated_at — created_at is the estimate's birth and can predate the booking by minutes to hours.

2

Link each conversion to its lead source

A conversion is only useful to Google Ads if we know which lead — and ideally which click (GCLID) — produced it. Sources in priority order:

1
CallRail tracked callsHAVE
Tracking numbers on every call. Match phone → estimate, pull GCLID from CallRail. Main source today.
2
Online bookingPARTIAL
Web form / booking-page leads. GA4 fire exists but needs GTM + GCLID capture on the form to attribute.
3
Google Local Services (LSA)GAP
Tracked only via CallRail today. Plan: integrate Google LSA directly for proper lead-level attribution.
4
Referral / organicGAP
No click to attribute. Need a tagging method (intake question, HCP lead-source field) so they're excluded cleanly, not misattributed.
5
Recurring / repeat customersPARTIAL
Return customers shouldn't count as new paid conversions. Detect repeat customer → suppress or send at $1 (as Repeat Phone Call already does).

Decisions to make

The forks the data won't decide for us. Mark these up and we lock them into the spec.

1. Should Scheduled & Qualified count toward Conversions, or stay observation-only?
Today both are include_in_conversions: false. Lean: keep observation-only — bid on Converted, watch the upper funnel. ☐ Keep ☐ Make biddable
2. LSA — keep CallRail-only, or build the Google LSA integration?
Lean: build the integration for proper lead-level attribution; CallRail under-counts LSA. ☐ Now ☐ Later ☐ CallRail-only
3. Recurring & referral leads — exclude from uploads, or report-only?
Lean: exclude from Google uploads, keep for internal reporting. Need a reliable repeat-customer + lead-source flag. ☐ Exclude ☐ Send at $1 ☐ Report-only
4. Counting type — leave Qualified/Converted as MANY_PER_CLICK, or tighten to ONE_PER_CLICK?
Many-per-click can inflate when one customer spawns several estimates/jobs. ☐ Keep many ☐ One-per-click
